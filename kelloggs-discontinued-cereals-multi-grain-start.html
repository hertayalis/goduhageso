THREE popular Kellogg's cereal flavors have been discontinued for years now, despite fans claiming they were great.

One of these flavors dates back to the mid-1980s and people are still talking about how amazing it was.

Kellogg's Multi-Grain Start was marketed as a healthy cereal for "active people."

It was produced in the United Kingdom until 2018 and fans are still distraught decades after it was removed from store shelves.

"Discontinued cereals that were amazing and whose absence makes the world a little bit less good. I'll Start," one Twitter user posted on Sunday along with a picture of the Multi-Grain Start box.

Fellow Twitter users were quick to agree with the cereal fan, commenting: "They were so good!"

To which the tweet creator said: "The absolute best."

"These were my go-to cereal," a third chimed in.

RICE KRISPIES TREATS

Another nostalgic cereal lover shared her favorite box of breakfast goodness with Twitter users on May 15.

She posted a picture of Kellogg's Rice Krispies Treats cereal, which was packed with rice krispies and marshmallow clusters.

"My favorite childhood cereal," the fan wrote. "Probably mid to late 90s."

"They brought it back not that long ago, but it was not the same at all ... and I think that one has already been discontinued as well," the Twitter user posted.

Originally launched in 1993, Rice Krispies Treats cereal made a comeback in 2019, but that recipe changed.

Instead of the Rice Krispies Treats clusters, the box contained mini marshmallows tossed into the original Rice Krispies cereal.

Disgruntled fans went as far as to sign an online petition, which received nearly 15,000 signatures, requesting Kellogg's to release the cereal using the original recipe.

Instead, Kellogg's discontinued the cereal altogether in 2020.

FIBERPLUS BERRY YOGURT CRUNCH

Another cereal that fans thought was delicious but didn't make the cut in Kellogg's factories was FiberPlus Berry Yogurt Crunch.

The cereal had wheat and rice flakes with clusters of berry-flavored yogurt.

It promoted health benefits like antioxidants and fiber. However, it only lasted three years in grocery stores.

The cereal was first released in 2010 but quickly discontinued in 2013.

One fan considered it his second-favorite cereal, according to a 2021 tweet.

In 2012, Twitter users had some great things to say about the cereal while it was still in circulation.
