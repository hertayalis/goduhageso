Inside Meghan Markle &amp; Prince Harry's Ibiza Hotel Vista Alegre

June 2024 · 2 minute read
  • Meghan Markle and Prince Harry reportedly traveled to Ibiza earlier this month following the duchess's 38th birthday on August 4.
  • The couple stayed in a villa at the seaside Vista Alegre complex, The Sun reported.

Prince Harry and Meghan, Duchess of Sussex, settled into some pretty sweet digs for their reported visit to Ibiza earlier this month. The couple and their three-month-old son, Archie Harrison Mountbatten-Windsor, jetted to the Spanish island for a getaway following Meghan's 38th birthday on August 4. The family of three reportedly stayed in a villa at the luxe gated community Vista Alegre, according to The Sun.

The exclusive vacation spot, dubbed a "billionaire's playground," boasts stunning seaside views and a fresh, modern design. Some properties include private beach access, an infinity pool, and live-in housekeepers. Not all prices for villa rentals are listed, but disclosed costs range from €3,500 to €22,000 (about $3,880 to $24,400) per week. Considering the Sussexes reportedly stayed for six days, they likely paid somewhere in that ballpark.

Local Spanish media first reported the Duke and Duchess of Sussex's visit to Ibiza last week. The trio traveled via private jet, which led some critics to call the couple "hypocrites" for using a not-so-sustainable mode of transportation as they fight climate change.

However, royal correspondent Omid Scobie reported that the Sussexes' flight was "carbon neutral," meaning they offset the plane's carbon footprint by supporting green initiatives that reduce carbon emissions elsewhere in the world.

The royals also flew carbon neutral when they traveled to Elton John's place in Nice, France, last week.
