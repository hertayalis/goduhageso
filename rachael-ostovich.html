Rachael Ostovich- Biography

Rachael Ostovich’s Professional Career

Rachael followed in her father’s footsteps and began her career as an MMA fighter. She began her career as an amateur in 2010, winning her first match 6-0 in Hawaii. Later that year, she competed in the Invicta Fighting Championship, where she won her debut bout against Evva Johnson. In 2017, she turned professional and competed in the Ultimate Fighting Championship, where she won 26 of the finals and lost the 27th. In 2019, she was set to face Paige Vanzant, but the match was rescheduled due to a domestic violence assault that damaged her orbital bone. The bout took place in January 2019, and she lost. She was slated to fight Shana Dobson at UFC Fight Night 168 in 2020, however, she backed out owing to a positive Ostarine test.

Rachael was then punished for a year by USADA after testing positive for Osterine, which was found in her supplements. She is a businesswoman that owns her own production company called Chi-Chi Beauty in addition to Martial Arts. In 2020, she developed a beauty line devoted to her daughter Ruby Rose. Rachael was unable to retain her form following her suspension in 2020, as she lost back-to-back fights before being released by the UFC in January 2020. She then began Bare Knuckle Boxing in 2021 and made her debut in the Bare Knuckle fighting championship in 2021 against Paige Vanzant, which she won 1-0.

Controversy

He punched her in the face, ribs, and head, causing her to vomit and cough up blood, as well as breaking her orbital bone. She, on the other hand, escaped through her balcony. She was also recently embroiled in a scandal after testing positive for Ostarine in her supplements. Her scheduled match was canceled, and she was punished for eight months by USADA for testing positive for illegal substances.
